The University of Cambodia

University of Cambodia (UC; Khmer: សាកលវិទ្យាល័យកម្ពុជា, UNGEGN: Sakâlôvĭtyéaloăy Kâmpŭchéa, ALA-LC: Sākalavidyālăy Kambujā) is a private[2] university located on North bridge Road in Sen Sok District, Phnom Penh, Kingdom of Cambodia.[3] Founded in 2003[4] by Dr. Kao Kim Hourn,[5] minister delegate to the prime minister of Cambodia,[6] and Dr. Haruhisa Handa,[7] founder and chairman of the International Foundation for Arts and Culture.[8] UC is accredited by the Royal Government of Cambodia[9] and the Accreditation Committee of Cambodia,[10] and is a member of the Cambodian Higher Education Association.[11]

university is organized to provide foundation year, associate, bachelor's, master's and doctoral programs through its twelve colleges and schools: the college of arts and humanities, the college of education, the college of law, the college of media and communications,[12] the college of science and technology, the college of social sciences, the school of creative arts, the school of foreign languages, the Techo Sen School of Government and International Relations,[13] and The Tony Fernandes School of Business (formerly the College of Management).[14] Additionally, the school of graduate studies oversees the university's graduate programs, and the school of undergraduate studies manages the undergraduate programs.[15]
